Instructions

All species you encounter are sometimes healthy and sometimes poisonous. However, you do not know how often mushrooms in each species are healthy or poisonous.

In addition, while the different ecosystems you explore vary in the types and frequency of mushrooms that grow there, poisonous mushrooms occur with the same overall frequency in all ecosystems. There are not some ecosystems where almost all the mushrooms are healthy and others where almost all are poisonous.

It is impossible for anyone but a trained expert to tell which species are usually healthy or usually poisonous just from appearance. Thus, even if two species appear similar, one may usually be healthy and the other may usually be poisonous. Also, how likely a certain species is to be poisonous will not change over the course of your hike.

Important: The only way to learn about whether a particular species is healthy or poisonous is to eat mushrooms of that species. The odds of a mushroom from a certain species being poisonous will not change over time. Different ecosystems vary in the type and frequency of mushrooms, but not in terms of the overall rate of poisonous items.
